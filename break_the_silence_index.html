Learn — Signs & Prevention

What is grooming?

Grooming is a process predators use to gain trust and manipulate young people to exploit them. It can happen in-person and online, and often starts with seeming kindness or attention.

  • Unusual secrecy about conversations or friendships
  • Someone asking for private photos or personal information
  • Rapid escalation of intimacy, gifts, or favors
  • Pressure to keep the relationship a secret

How to protect yourself & others

  • Keep privacy settings strong on social platforms and never share personal location details.
  • Be cautious with strangers online — verify identities before trusting them.
  • Talk openly with a trusted adult if anything feels wrong. You won’t get in trouble for asking for help.
  • Save evidence (screenshots, messages) and reach out to authorities or hotlines if you or someone you know is in danger.

About

Our purpose

Break the Silence exists to give people clear, gentle information about how predators operate, to help survivors find trusted support, and to encourage communities to protect young people through education and care.

Why I Made This Website

"Break the Silence" was created after I learned about disturbing incidents of grooming and exploitation involving people in online communities. Seeing how easily trust can be abused made me realize how many young people are affected by predators — and how few know how to protect themselves or where to find help. This site exists to spread awareness, educate, and support survivors. My goal is to help others recognize the warning signs and to remind every victim that they are not alone, and healing is always possible.
